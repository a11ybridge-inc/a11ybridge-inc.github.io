About A11YBRIDGE

We believe accessibility is a human right — and that digital products should work for every person, in every market.

Our mission

Bridging the accessibility gap — across borders, sectors, and languages

A11YBRIDGE was founded on a simple belief: inclusive digital products are not a luxury, they are a baseline. Every person — regardless of ability, language, or location — deserves access to the digital tools and information that shape daily life.

We partner with government agencies, educational institutions, nonprofits, and corporate teams across the GCC, Africa, and the Middle East to build accessible digital products from the inside out. That means auditing what exists, training the people who build it, and embedding accessibility into how organizations think and work — not just bolting it on at the end.

How we work

Our values in practice

01

Lead by example

Every document, website, and deliverable we produce is itself WCAG-compliant. We don't just advise on accessibility — we demonstrate it in everything we create.

02

Friendly, non-judgmental

We meet organizations where they are — not where they should be. Every engagement starts with curiosity and ends with clarity, confidence, and a clear path forward.

03

Context-aware expertise

We understand that GCC, African, and Middle Eastern markets have unique regulatory, linguistic, and cultural dimensions. Our approach is calibrated to your actual context — not a copy-paste from a Western playbook.

04

Pragmatic and actionable

We prioritize findings and recommendations so your team can make real progress immediately — not get paralyzed by a 200-page report of issues with no clear starting point.

05

Committed to inclusion

Accessibility is a technical discipline, but it's also deeply human. We keep the people it serves — people with disabilities — at the center of everything we recommend.

06

Standards-rigorous

We work to WCAG 2.2, EN 301 549, Section 508, and ISO/IEC 40500. Our recommendations are grounded in recognized international standards — defensible in procurement and regulatory contexts.

Why this region

The GCC, Africa & the Middle East are where accessibility momentum is building

Across the GCC, national digital transformation programs are driving explosive growth in government and enterprise digital products — and accessibility legislation is beginning to follow. Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Qatar are accelerating accessibility requirements for public sector digital services. Organizations that build in accessibility now will have a significant compliance and competitive advantage.

Across Africa, mobile-first digital infrastructure is expanding rapidly — but too often without inclusive design. Disability affects a significant proportion of the population across the continent, yet digital products rarely account for this reality. We help organizations in education, health, and fintech lead the region on inclusive digital product development.

Arabic language support adds a unique layer of complexity: right-to-left layout, proper keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility with Arabic text, and culturally appropriate communication. We bring specific expertise in RTL accessibility and Arabic-language WCAG compliance.

We work with government ministries, international development organizations, universities, banks, health platforms, and NGOs — providing accessibility expertise that is grounded in international standards but calibrated to the realities and opportunities of the region.
